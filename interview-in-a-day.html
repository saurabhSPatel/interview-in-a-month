01System Design Interview — Staff Framework

MIN 0–3
Clarify Requirements (you ask, they answer)

Who are the users? What scale — DAU, requests/sec? Read vs write ratio? Latency SLA (p99)? Consistency requirements? Any special constraints? Global or single-region? Don't design until you know this. Staff engineers are expected to discover requirements, not wait for them.

MIN 3–8
Capacity Estimation + Back-of-Envelope

100M DAU × 10 actions/day = 1B actions/day = ~12K RPS. Storage: 100M users × 1KB profile = 100GB. Know: 1 day = 86K seconds (round to 100K). Bandwidth: 12K RPS × 100KB = 1.2 GB/s. This sets the conversation — design for this scale.

MIN 8–20
High-Level Design — Draw the Box Diagram

Client → LB → API gateway → Services → Storage. Be specific: "a read-optimized service" → "a Go microservice with connection pool to Cassandra, caches in Redis with 5-min TTL". Name technologies. Explain choices. Staff engineers commit to decisions with reasoning.

MIN 20–35
Deep Dive — Pick the Hardest Component

Interviewer will ask — or you should volunteer: "The hardest part is message delivery guarantees. Let me go deep on that." Show you can go from HLD to LLD. Explain the data model, API contracts, failure modes. This is where Staff-level depth shows.

MIN 35–45
Trade-offs + Scaling + Follow-ups

Proactively say: "This design has a bottleneck at X. At 10x scale I'd shard by Y. Currently we'd hit the single Kafka partition at 1M msgs/sec, so we'd partition by account_id." Interviewers love engineers who identify their own design's weaknesses.

Signs of Staff vs Senior in System Design

Senior Signals ✓

  • Correctly identifies main components
  • Knows common patterns (LB, cache, DB)
  • Responds to interviewer hints
  • Can implement what they design

Staff Signals ✓✓

  • Drives the conversation proactively
  • Identifies multiple valid approaches + trade-offs
  • Connects design to business requirements
  • Proactively surfaces failure modes
  • Knows when NOT to use a technology

02Behavioral — STAR Stories from Your Work

Your experience is excellent for Staff-level stories. Here are pre-built STAR templates using your actual work.

Q: Tell me about a time you made an architectural decision with significant trade-offs.
Situation

Building CBDC interoperable bridge — needed to connect multiple blockchain networks in real-time.

Task

Design the cross-network asset transfer system with guaranteed delivery and double-spend prevention.

Action

Chose Kafka-based event sourcing with exactly-once semantics. Considered 2PC but rejected for availability. Implemented Saga pattern with compensating transactions. Chose CP over AP for CBDC's zero-tolerance for double spend.

Result

99.99% uptime, zero double-spends in production. Trade-off: higher latency during Kafka broker unavailability — acceptable for financial system.

Signals: technical depth, explicit trade-off reasoning, business context awareness
Q: Tell me about a time you improved system performance significantly.
Situation

MariaDB query load spiking during forex settlement, causing latency spikes in peak trading hours.

Task

Reduce DB load without data accuracy loss for financial transactions.

Action

Analyzed query patterns → forex rates read 100:1 vs write. Designed Redis cache with time-bucketed keys (forex:rate:USDEUR:15min), cache-aside pattern, TTL aligned to rate feed update frequency. Added event-driven invalidation via Kafka for real-time rate changes.

Result

80% reduction in DB load. P99 latency dropped from 200ms to 15ms. Zero cache staleness incidents — TTL + event invalidation covered all cases.

Signals: measurement-driven, layered solution (not just "added cache"), quantified results
Q: Describe a time you had to lead without authority.
Situation

Multi-org Hyperledger Fabric deployment — each bank had separate DevOps teams with different deployment preferences.

Task

Standardize deployment across 5 participant banks without authority over their infrastructure teams.

Action

Designed Helm chart that was composable — each bank could override values while sharing core chart. Ran workshops demonstrating 60% faster deployment. Created runbook for their ops teams. Made it easy to adopt, not mandatory.

Result

4/5 banks adopted within 2 months. 60% deployment time reduction. One bank held out — respected their decision, kept compatibility. Cross-team trust built.

Signals: influence without authority, empathy for other teams, pragmatism (not mandating)

03Staff-Level Question Bank

Architecture Questions

  • "How would you migrate a monolith to microservices?"
  • "How do you handle distributed transactions?"
  • "When would you NOT use microservices?"
  • "How do you ensure consistency across services?"
  • "How do you design for 10x traffic in 6 months?"

Leadership Questions

  • "How do you resolve technical disagreements?"
  • "Tell me about a time you changed course mid-project"
  • "How do you balance tech debt vs new features?"
  • "How do you mentor junior engineers?"
  • "How do you influence decisions above your level?"

Deep Technical

  • "Kafka exactly-once — how does it actually work?"
  • "You have a split brain in your DB cluster — what now?"
  • "How do you detect memory leaks in a Go service?"
  • "Explain your understanding of strong consistency"
  • "How would you debug a service with rising p99 latency?"

04Common Staff Interview Traps

❌ Trap: Jumping to solution without requirements
Fix: Always spend 3-5 minutes clarifying. Interviewers penalize engineers who design before understanding. Say "Before I design, I want to understand the usage patterns — is this read or write heavy?"
❌ Trap: Using buzzwords without depth ("I'd use Kubernetes")
Fix: Always explain the why. "I'd containerize with Docker and deploy on Kubernetes because we need horizontal scaling for the stateless API layer, and K8s HPA handles traffic spikes automatically with Prometheus metrics."
❌ Trap: Proposing a perfect system with no trade-offs
Fix: All systems have trade-offs. Proactively say: "This design optimizes for availability at the cost of strong consistency — which is acceptable for this feed use case but wouldn't work for payments."
❌ Trap: Behavioral answers without data ("I improved performance")
Fix: Always quantify. "Reduced P99 from 200ms to 15ms" not "made it faster". "Reduced deployment time by 60%" not "made deployments faster".
❌ Trap: Waiting for interviewer to ask about trade-offs
Fix: Staff engineers proactively surface weaknesses. "One limitation of this approach is..." shows intellectual honesty and system-level thinking.

✓ Have 3 STAR stories ready: impact, trade-off decision, conflict/disagreement. From your CBDC, ForEx, Fabric work.
✓ Practice: "Tell me about yourself" in exactly 90 seconds. Senior SWE → distributed systems → CBDC at scale → Staff trajectory.
